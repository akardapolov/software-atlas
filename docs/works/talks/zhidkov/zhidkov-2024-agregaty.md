# Агрегаты

## Overview

A comprehensive explanation of **aggregates** from Domain-Driven Design — a concept Zhidkov considers the foundation of maintainable information systems, though it remains relatively unknown outside the DDD community.

## Key Points

### Why Aggregates Matter

Aggregates from DDD lie at the foundation of maintainable systems. However:
- The concept is little-known outside DDD communities
- It's difficult to understand
- This article aims to structure understanding and create a "guidebook" for teams

### What Is an Aggregate?

The article provides:
- Clear definition of aggregates
- Explanation of their role in domain modeling
- Practical examples of aggregate boundaries
- Guidance on identifying aggregates in real systems

### Aggregates in the Ergonomic Approach

Within the Ergonomic Approach:
- Domain is split into small, loosely-coupled aggregates
- Aggregates and relationships form a directed acyclic graph (DAG)
- Aggregates are immutable objects stored in mutable repositories
