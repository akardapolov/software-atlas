## Summary

Forsgren/Humble/Kim conducted a massive study (State of DevOps)
and showed: high-performing teams differ not by "heroism" but by
a system of technical and organizational practices.

The book popularized **DORA metrics** — 4 key delivery metrics:

1. **Deployment Frequency**
2. **Lead Time for Changes**
3. **Change Failure Rate**
4. **Time to Restore Service**

## Key Ideas

### 1) Measure flow, not busyness

Development effectiveness is the speed and reliability of value delivery,
not the number of tasks "in progress."

### 2) Capabilities that correlate with outcomes

Examples of strong predictors:
- Continuous Delivery (automation, small batch)
- Trunk-based development
- Test automation
- Observability / monitoring
- Westrum culture (generative culture)
- Low cognitive load, platform approaches

### 3) False dichotomies debunked

High speed ≠ low stability.
Research shows: best in speed are usually best in reliability too.

## Impact and Legacy

- DORA metrics became an industry standard
- DevOps became "measurable"
- the connection between engineering practices and business results became provable
