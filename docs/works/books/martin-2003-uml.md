## Summary

UML Distilled is a concise guide to the Unified Modeling Language, providing a practical subset of UML diagrams most useful for software development.

## Key Ideas

1. **Minimal UML** — you don't need all UML diagrams; use only what adds value
2. **Communication over ceremony** — diagrams exist to communicate ideas, not for their own sake
3. **UML as sketch** — informal diagrams are often more useful than formal models

## Historical Context

UML emerged from the unification of Booch, OMT, and OOSE methods in the mid-1990s. By the early 2000s, it had become the industry standard for object-oriented modeling.

## Impact and Legacy

UML Distilled became one of the most popular UML references because of its pragmatic approach to modeling.
