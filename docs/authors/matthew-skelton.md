## Biography

Matthew Skelton is a researcher, consultant, and author known for co-authoring *Team Topologies* (2019) with Manuel Pais. Skelton's work focuses on how team structures affect software delivery and how organizations can design teams to optimize flow.

Skelton's research and insights emerged from extensive work with organizations across different industries, helping teams understand how team topology impacts delivery performance and organizational health.

## Key Contributions

### Team Topologies (2019)

With Manuel Pais, Skelton identified four fundamental team types that optimize for different contexts:

| Team Type | Purpose | Interaction Mode |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| **Stream-aligned** | Deliver value on business stream | Primary team type |
| **Enabling** | Help stream teams overcome obstacles | Temporary, 1-2 sprints |
| **Complicated Subsystem** | Own complex, specialized domains | X-as-a-Service |
| **Platform** | Provide self-service infrastructure | X-as-a-Service |

**Key insights:**

1. **Stream-aligned teams are the primary unit of value delivery**
   - These teams work directly on business capabilities
   - Should be protected from disruption

2. **Other team types serve stream-aligned teams**
   - Enabling teams accelerate learning and reduce bottlenecks
   - Platform and Complicated Subsystem teams provide X-as-a-Service capabilities

3. **Team structure should match desired architecture**
   - Skelton & Pais: "Inverse Conway Maneuver" — design teams to get the architecture you want
   - Conway's Law in reverse: organization structure influences system structure

4. **Team size matters**
   - Dunbar's number: cognitive limit of stable relationships (~150 people)
   - Amazon's two-pizza teams: optimal size for communication overhead

5. **Cognitive load should be managed**
   - Too many dependencies → increase cognitive load
   - Clear team boundaries → reduce coordination overhead
   - Match communication patterns to work type
