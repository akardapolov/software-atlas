## Biography

Adele Goldberg was a computer scientist and key figure in the development of **Smalltalk-80** at Xerox PARC. She co-authored the canonical Smalltalk documentation — the "Blue Book" and "Orange Book" — that became the primary way the world learned about Smalltalk.

Goldberg played a crucial role in making Smalltalk accessible beyond Xerox PARC. She insisted on publishing the language and its environment, overcoming internal resistance. Her work directly led to the founding of **ParcPlace Systems**, which commercialized Smalltalk.

## Key Contributions

### Smalltalk-80 Documentation

Goldberg co-authored two definitive works on Smalltalk:

- **"Smalltalk-80: The Language and Its Implementation"** (1983, with David Robson) — The Blue Book
- **"Smalltalk-80: The Interactive Programming Environment"** (1984) — The Orange Book

These books provided:
- Complete language reference and semantics
- Explanation of the virtual machine architecture
- Description of the integrated development environment (class browser, debugger, inspector)

### Publication and Commercialization

At Xerox PARC, Goldberg:
- **Insisted on publishing** Smalltalk-80 despite internal opposition
- **Founded ParcPlace Systems** to commercialize Smalltalk
- Ensured Smalltalk ideas reached the broader programming community

Without her advocacy, Smalltalk might have remained an internal Xerox research project, and its influence on modern languages and IDEs would have been significantly diminished.
