## Biography

Tom B. Brown is a machine learning researcher and the lead author of
the GPT-3 paper ("Language Models are Few-Shot Learners," 2020). At
the time, he was at OpenAI, where he helped demonstrate that scaling
language models to 175 billion parameters unlocks **in-context learning**
— the ability to learn new tasks from a few examples in the prompt.

## Key Contributions

### GPT-3 and In-Context Learning (2020)

Brown led the paper that introduced GPT-3 and demonstrated its ability
to perform tasks via few-shot prompting — without any gradient updates
or fine-tuning. This established prompting as the primary interface to
large language models.

The paper showed that model capabilities improve smoothly with scale
up to a point, then show qualitative jumps — a pattern later called
"emergence."

### Subsequent Work

Brown has continued research on language model capabilities, alignment,
and the scaling properties of neural networks.
