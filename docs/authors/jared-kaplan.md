## Biography

Jared Kaplan is a theoretical physicist turned machine learning
researcher. He holds a PhD in physics from Stanford and worked as a
professor at Johns Hopkins before moving to AI research.

Kaplan was a key member of the team at OpenAI and later Anthropic that
established the empirical **scaling laws** for language models — the
power-law relationships between model size, data, compute, and
performance.

## Key Contributions

### Scaling Laws (2020)

Kaplan led the paper that demonstrated language model performance
follows predictable power laws. The key finding: loss scales as a power
law with model size, dataset size, and compute independently. This
enabled researchers to predict the performance of large models from
small experiments.

The practical implication: given a fixed compute budget, there are
optimal ways to allocate parameters versus training tokens. This
informed the design of GPT-3, GPT-4, and subsequent models.

### Theoretical Physics Background

Kaplan's background in theoretical physics (quantum field theory,
string theory) informed his approach to scaling — treating neural
networks as statistical systems with emergent properties.
