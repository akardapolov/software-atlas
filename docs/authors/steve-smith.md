# Steve Smith

| | |
|---|---|
| **Fields** | DevOps, software delivery, DORA metrics |

## Biography

Steve Smith is a researcher and author known for co-authoring *Accelerate* (2018), *What Drives DevOps Performance* (2020), and *A Set of Principles for High-Performing Teams* (2021) with Nicole Forsgren, Gene Kim, Jez Humble, and David Farley. Smith is a research scientist and consultant, and co-founder of Humble, Farley & Kim.

Smith's research focuses on understanding the organizational and cultural factors that enable high-performance software delivery teams, contributing to the DORA (DevOps Research and Assessment) body of knowledge.

## Key Contributions

### Accelerate Research (2018)

With Nicole Forsgren, Gene Kim, Jez Humble, and David Farley, Smith contributed to establishing the empirical foundation for DevOps research:

**Key organizational factors:**
- **Psychological safety** — Freedom to experiment without fear
- **Team empowerment** — Autonomy and decision-making authority
- **Clear vision** — Product direction and roadmap alignment
- **Cross-functional collaboration** — Effective communication across teams

### What Drives DevOps Performance (2020)

With Nicole Forsgren, Smith helped identify the key drivers of software delivery performance:

**Technology factors:**
- **Version control systems** — Main branch vs. feature branches
- **Automated testing** — Test coverage and automated test runs
- **Deployment automation** — Self-service deployment capabilities
- **Monitoring and observability** — Real-time metrics and alerting

**Process factors:**
- **Agile practices** — Small batches, rapid feedback
- **Architecture** — Loosely coupled, modular systems
- **Postmortems** — Learning-focused, blameless

### A Set of Principles for High-Performing Teams (2021)

With Nicole Forsgren, Gene Kim, Smith documented key principles that distinguish elite teams:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Technical excellence** | Strong engineering practices, architectural decisions |
| **Product vision** | Clear direction, measurable goals, success metrics |
| **Autonomy** | Team empowerment, local decision-making |
| **Psychological safety** | Learning culture, blameless postmortems |
| **Cross-functional collaboration** | Effective teamwork across organizational boundaries |
