## Biography

Kristen Nygaard was a Norwegian computer scientist and political activist
who, together with Ole-Johan Dahl, created **Simula**. While Dahl focused
on language design and implementation, Nygaard brought the conceptual
framework from his background in operations research — the idea that
software should model real-world systems and their interactions.

Nygaard worked at the Norwegian Computing Center (NCC) from 1960, where
Simula was developed. He later became professor at the University of Oslo.
Beyond programming languages, Nygaard was deeply involved in Scandinavian
**participatory design** — the movement arguing that workers should
participate in designing the computer systems that affect their work.

He and Dahl received the Turing Award in 2001, and both died in 2002.

## Key Contributions

### Simula — Conceptual Vision

Nygaard's background in operations research gave him a particular way of
thinking about systems: as collections of interacting entities, each with
their own state and behaviour. When existing languages couldn't express
this naturally, he partnered with Dahl to create one that could.

Nygaard provided:

- **The problem domain** — simulation of real-world systems
- **The conceptual framework** — objects as models of real entities
- **The philosophy** — that programming languages shape how we think about problems

### Participatory Design

Nygaard believed that people affected by computer systems should have
a voice in their design. He was instrumental in the Scandinavian
participatory design movement, which influenced:

- User-centered design
- Agile's emphasis on customer collaboration
- Domain-Driven Design's focus on domain experts

### The BETA Language (1980s)

Nygaard later co-designed BETA, a successor to Simula that unified
classes, procedures, and types into a single concept called a "pattern."
While BETA was not widely adopted, it explored ideas later seen in other
languages.

## Influence

### Influenced by

- **Operations research** — modelling and simulation methodology
- **Trade union movement** — workers' rights to influence technology

### Influenced

- **Alan Kay** — Simula inspired Smalltalk
- **Bjarne Stroustrup** — C++ directly descends from Simula's class model
- **Participatory design movement** — human-centered system design
- **Eric Evans** — DDD's emphasis on domain modelling echoes Nygaard's philosophy
