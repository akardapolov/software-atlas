## Biography

John McCarthy was an American computer scientist and one of the founding
figures of artificial intelligence. He studied mathematics at Caltech and
Princeton, later holding positions at MIT and Stanford.

McCarthy is best known for two things that each would be enough for a major
career: he **coined the term "artificial intelligence"** in the 1950s and
created **Lisp**, one of the most influential programming languages ever made.

His work combined mathematical logic with practical computing. He believed
that symbolic reasoning could be mechanised and that computers could be used
not just for numerical calculation but for manipulation of ideas, expressions,
and knowledge.

## Key Contributions

### Lisp (1958)

McCarthy created **Lisp** as a practical notation for symbolic computation,
drawing inspiration from lambda calculus.

Lisp introduced or popularised many ideas that later became mainstream:

- Recursion as a central programming tool
- Garbage collection
- Symbolic computation
- Higher-order functions
- Interactive REPL-driven development
- Code-as-data (homoiconicity)

Lisp became the foundational language of AI research for decades and also
the ancestor of Scheme, Common Lisp, Clojure, Racket, and many ideas in ML
and Haskell.

### Artificial Intelligence as a Field

McCarthy was one of the organisers of the 1956 Dartmouth workshop, often
considered the birth event of AI as a formal research field.

By naming the field **artificial intelligence**, he helped define its
identity and ambitions: building machines capable of reasoning, learning,
and problem-solving.

### Time-Sharing

McCarthy also advocated for **time-sharing systems**, in which many users
share a computer interactively. This was an important step away from the
batch-processing model and toward modern interactive computing.

### Logic and Formal Representation

Throughout his career, McCarthy worked on formal approaches to common-sense
reasoning, knowledge representation, and logic-based AI.

## Influence

### Influenced by

- **Alonzo Church** — lambda calculus directly shaped Lisp's conceptual core

### Influenced

- **Lisp family languages** — Scheme, Common Lisp, Clojure, Racket
- **AI research** — symbolic AI, logic programming, representation of knowledge
- **Programming language design** — macros, REPLs, symbolic computation

## Why McCarthy Matters

McCarthy helped move computing beyond arithmetic. He showed that programs
could manipulate **symbols, rules, and expressions**, not just numbers.
That shift opened the path to AI, symbolic programming, and much of modern
language research.
