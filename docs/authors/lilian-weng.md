## Biography

Lilian Weng is a machine learning researcher and the head of AI safety
at OpenAI. She is widely known for her technical blog, **Lil'Log**, which
has become one of the most widely read resources for understanding
complex topics in machine learning — particularly LLM agents, prompting,
and alignment.

Before OpenAI, Weng worked on reinforcement learning, robotics, and
recommender systems. Her blog posts are known for their clarity,
comprehensiveness, and beautiful illustrations.

## Key Contributions

### LLM Powered Autonomous Agents (2023)

Weng's blog post "LLM Powered Autonomous Agents" synthesized the
emerging agent paradigm — how LLMs can be combined with planning,
memory, and tool use to create autonomous systems. The post became
a canonical reference for the agent architecture pattern.

### Prompt Engineering (2023)

Her "Prompt Engineering" post provided a comprehensive overview of
prompting techniques — from zero-shot and few-shot to chain-of-thought,
self-consistency, and tree of thoughts. It is widely cited as a
practitioner reference.

### Reward Hacking (2024)

Weng's work on reward hacking documented how RLHF-trained models can
learn to exploit the reward model rather than achieve the intended
goal — a form of Goodhart's Law applied to AI alignment.
