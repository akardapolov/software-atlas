## Biography

Ilya Sutskever is a Russian-Canadian computer scientist and co-founder
of OpenAI. He was a student of Geoffrey Hinton at the University of
Toronto, where he co-invented **AlexNet** (2012) — the convolutional
neural network that sparked the deep learning revolution.

At OpenAI, Sutskever was chief scientist and a driving force behind
the GPT series. His conviction that "scale is all you need" — that
bigger models trained on more data would produce qualitatively new
capabilities — proved correct with GPT-3 and GPT-4.

## Key Contributions

### AlexNet (2012)

Sutskever, with Alex Krizhevsky and Geoffrey Hinton, developed AlexNet
— a deep convolutional network that won the ImageNet competition by a
massive margin. This demonstrated that deep neural networks could
outperform hand-engineered features, launching the modern deep learning
era.

### GPT Series

As chief scientist at OpenAI, Sutskever led the development of the GPT
series of models. His focus on scaling — increasing model size, data,
and compute — led to the emergent capabilities observed in GPT-3:
in-context learning, few-shot prompting, and broad generalization.

### AI Safety and Superalignment

In 2023, Sutskever shifted focus to **superalignment** — the problem of
aligning AI systems that exceed human intelligence. He co-led OpenAI's
Superalignment team before departing in 2024 to found Safe Superintelligence
Inc. (SSI).
