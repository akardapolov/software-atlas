## Biography

Ivan Edward Sutherland is an American computer scientist and Internet pioneer, widely regarded as one of the most important figures in the history of computer graphics. His 1963 PhD thesis **"Sketchpad"** revolutionized human-computer interaction and introduced concepts that would later influence object-oriented programming.

While at MIT, Sutherland developed Sketchpad — the first interactive computer-aided design system. It introduced the concept of **master drawings** that could be instantiated multiple times, each instance independently modifiable. This was one of the earliest manifestations of what would become the **class-instance** relationship in OOP.

Sutherland received the Turing Award in 1988 "for his pioneering and visionary contributions to computer graphics, starting with Sketchpad, and continuing after."

## Key Contributions

### Sketchpad (1963)

Sketchpad was the first computer system that allowed users to create and manipulate graphical objects directly on screen. Key innovations:

- **Master drawings** — templates from which instances were created (precursor to classes)
- **Instances** — copies of masters that could be modified independently (precursor to objects)
- **Constraints** — objects maintained their own rules (precursor to encapsulation and invariants)
- **Direct manipulation** — users worked with objects directly, not through code

This system was Alan Kay's inspiration for seeing that **objects were a natural way to think about graphics**. When Kay saw Sketchpad as a graduate student at University of Utah, it was one of the three key influences on his vision of OOP (along with Simula and Lisp).

### Other Contributions

- **Head-mounted display** — first VR headset (1968)
- **Computer-aided design (CAD)** — foundational work in the field
- **Academic leadership** — led research groups at Harvard, Utah, and Caltech

## Influence

### Influenced by

- **Claude Shannon** — information theory
- **Vannevar Bush** — Memex concept

### Influenced

- **Alan Kay** — Sketchpad's master/instance concept directly inspired Kay's vision of OOP
- **Computer graphics industry** — Sketchpad established the foundation for all modern CAD and graphics systems
- **Direct manipulation interfaces** — GUI concepts emerged from Sketchpad's interaction model
