## Biography

Manuel Pais is a researcher, consultant, and author known for co-authoring *Team Topologies* (2019) with Matthew Skelton. Pais's work focuses on organizational design, team structure, and how these factors influence software delivery and team performance.

Pais brings extensive practical experience from working with organizations across different industries, helping teams understand and apply principles of effective team organization.

## Key Contributions

### Team Topologies (2019)

With Matthew Skelton, Pais developed a comprehensive framework for understanding team structures and their impact on software delivery:

| Team Type | Purpose | Interaction Mode |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| **Stream-aligned** | Deliver value on business stream | Primary team type |
| **Enabling** | Help stream teams overcome obstacles | Temporary, 1-2 sprints |
| **Complicated Subsystem** | Own complex, specialized domains | X-as-a-Service |
| **Platform** | Provide self-service infrastructure | X-as-a-Service |

**Key framework elements:**

1. **Inverse Conway Maneuver**
   - Design team structure to achieve desired system architecture
   - Conway's Law in reverse: structure follows communication
   - Deliberate team topology as an architectural decision

2. **Cognitive Load Management**
   - Understanding Dunbar's number (~150 stable relationships)
   - Minimizing coordination overhead through clear boundaries
   - Reducing cognitive load through limiting dependencies

3. **Team Interaction Patterns**
   - Collaboration: team members work together temporarily
   - X-as-a-Service: clear interface, minimal deep collaboration
   - Facilitating: one team accelerates another's learning

4. **Flow Optimization**
   - Reducing handoffs and bottlenecks
   - Matching team structure to delivery patterns
   - Protecting stream-aligned teams from disruption

5. **Team Size and Structure**
   - Guidelines for optimal team composition
   - Balancing autonomy with coordination
   - Alignment between team types and business goals
